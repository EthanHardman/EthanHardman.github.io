Live · Mixing & Mastering

WELL Said – 40th Anniversary Live at The Met

A ska / pop / funk band with full rhythm section and horns: electric guitars, bass, drums, saxophones, trumpet, trombone and vocals. Recorded live at The Met in Bury for a multi-camera video release.

What this is

  • 2.5-hour anniversary gig for WELL Said
  • Multi-mic live band recording at The Met, Bury
  • Mixed and mastered for YouTube-style video release

What I did

  • Gain staging and cleanup across all sources
  • EQ & compression for drums, bass, guitars, horns & vocals
  • Parallel processing to keep energy without harshness
  • Balancing crowd / room mics with the close mics

What to listen for

  • How the horns stay bright without becoming piercing
  • Kick / bass relationship in the low end
  • Vocals staying intelligible over a dense live mix
  • Audience ambience that feels present but controlled

About

I’m Ethan Hardman, a UK-based mixing & mastering engineer, producer and trumpet player. I’ve been playing trumpet since 2011, performing in orchestras, big bands and brass bands, and I currently play front row cornet with Lindley Brass Band in West Yorkshire.

I hold a BA in Music from the University of Leeds and a Level 3 BTEC National Extended Certificate in Music Technology. I’ve been working in Ableton Live since 2019, with additional experience in Logic, and my workflow is built around FabFilter, Serum and detailed referencing.

My background as an instrumentalist means I mix with a musician’s ear: pitch, timing and feel matter just as much as loudness and tone.
