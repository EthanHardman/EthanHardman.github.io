Services

How I work on your music

I enjoy producing UK electronic styles, but my favourite thing to mix and master is music with real instruments and human feel – where my background on trumpet makes it easier to lock into the performance.

Mixing

I mix full multi-track sessions for both live and electronic music. Whether it’s a band recording, a project full of layered synths or a hybrid of the two, I try to make everything feel coherent and musical.

  • Clean, balanced, powerful mixes with an ear for performance and feel.
  • Extra attention to live instrumentation, phrasing and dynamics.
  • Low-end control and width for electronic tracks without losing groove.

Mastering

My mastering focuses on keeping the musical intention intact while getting your track to sit comfortably next to other releases.

  • Loudness without flattening everything into a brick.
  • Masters that work on headphones, speakers and live systems.
  • Streaming-ready levels and optional DJ-friendly versions.

Vocal Editing

Vocals are often the emotional centre of a track, so I treat them carefully.

  • Breath and noise clean-up where it helps clarity.
  • Subtle tuning and timing tweaks that still feel natural.
  • Tone shaping so the vocal sits in the mix without fighting everything else.

Production Help & WIPs

If you’re too close to the project, I can help you get unstuck and move forwards.

  • Arrangement suggestions and structure clean-up.
  • Sound selection and drum/groove refinement.
  • Taking a rough idea or loop and shaping it into a finished piece.

All paid services go through Fiverr so payment and file delivery stay safe and organised. If I have availability for free/portfolio work, that’s arranged directly over email.

Process

How working with me usually feels

I try to keep things simple: clear communication, musical decisions, and enough structure that you always know what’s happening next.

  1. 1

    Share your track & goals

    You send me a demo or rough mix, a bit of context, and what you’d like to improve – maybe the drums don’t hit hard enough, the vocal feels buried, or a live recording needs more polish.

  2. 2

    Plan & quote

    I’ll reply with first impressions, suggestions and a plan. If it’s a paid project, I’ll direct you to Fiverr where we can lock it in properly with a clear scope and price.

  3. 3

    Mixing / mastering & revisions

    I’ll work through the mix or master, then send you a version to listen to in your own space. Your feedback is part of the process – I’d rather know what you actually feel than guess.

  4. 4

    Delivery & formats

    Once we’re both happy, I’ll deliver final files in the formats you need – streaming, DJ-friendly masters, high-res WAV and so on. My goal is for you to feel confident pressing play anywhere.

About me

Musician first, engineer by choice.

I’m an audio engineer and music producer based in Greater Manchester, UK. I’ve been working with audio since 2019, and I come at everything from a musical angle rather than purely technical.

I studied BA (Hons) Music at the University of Leeds and BTEC Music Technology, and I’ve been playing trumpet since 2011. That performance background really shapes how I hear things – especially live instrumentation and arrangements where phrasing and dynamics matter.

I do enjoy producing UK-based electronic genres, but when I’m mixing and mastering I’m particularly drawn to music with real instruments and human feel. Brass, bands, acoustic recordings – that’s where my ear feels most at home.
